Layman’s Guide to Non-Locality

Non-locality is when two things, like particles, seem to affect each other instantly, no matter how far apart they are. It’s a puzzle because Einstein’s relativity says nothing can move faster than light, but quantum entanglement suggests particles “talk” without delay. With Silver Phoenix, we’ve found a way to explain this using our logic gate and dimensional relationships, showing how the universe’s fractal structure might allow instant connections without breaking physics.
